Lab Notes

Agglutination

If a recipient has antibodies against a donor's antigens, the blood will clump. This is why O- can give to anyone; it has no antigens to "trip" the alarm.

Rh Factor

Positive blood has the Rhesus protein. Negatives don't. A negative person's immune system will attack the Rh protein if they receive positive blood.
